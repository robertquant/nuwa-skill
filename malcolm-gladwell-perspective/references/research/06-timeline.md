# Malcolm Gladwell Timeline
## Complete Biographical Timeline

---

## Early Life (1963-1987)

### Birth and Family
- **September 3, 1963**: Born in Fareham, Hampshire, England
  - Father: Graham Gladwell (British mathematics professor)
  - Mother: Joyce Gladwell (née Nation, Jamaican-born psychotherapist and author)
  - Family moved to Canada when Malcolm was 6 years old

### Education
- **1969**: Family settled in Elmira, Ontario, Canada
- **Early Education**: Grew up in rural Ontario
- **1982**: Graduated from high school in Ontario
- **1982-1987**: Attended University of Toronto's Trinity College
  - Graduated with a Bachelor's degree in History
  - During college, was an avid runner and middle-distance athlete

---

## Washington Post Period (1987-1996)

### Journalism Career Begins
- **1987**: Joined The Washington Post as a science writer
  - Covered business and science beats
  - Developed his signature storytelling style
- **Early 1990s**: Became the newspaper's New York City bureau chief
  - Covered economics, technology, and social issues
  - Began developing ideas that would later become "The Tipping Point"

### Key Developments
- **1993**: Wrote influential article about the concept of "tipping points" for The New Yorker (later expanded into book)
- **1996**: Left The Washington Post to join The New Yorker as a staff writer
  - **🔴 MILESTONE**: Transition from journalist to author-influencer

---

## The New Yorker & Author Period (1996-Present)

### The New Yorker Years
- **1996**: Joined The New Yorker as staff writer
  - Created some of his most influential articles
  - Developed concepts for future books

### Book Publications

#### The Tipping Point (2000)
- **Publication Date**: March 2000
- **Background**: Explored how small changes can create major social shifts
- **🔴 MILESTONE**: First book, became international bestseller
- **Impact**: Introduced concepts like "connectors," "mavens," and "salesmen"

#### Blink: The Power of Thinking Without Thinking (2005)
- **Publication Date**: January 2005
- **Background**: Explores snap judgments and rapid cognition
- **Impact**: Popularized understanding of unconscious decision-making
- **🔴 MILESTONE**: Second #1 New York Times bestseller

#### Outliers: The Story of Success (2008)
- **Publication Date**: November 2008
- **Background**: Examines factors contributing to extraordinary success
- **Key Concept**: "10,000-Hour Rule" for mastery
- **🔴 MILESTONE**: Third consecutive bestseller, cemented public intellectual status

#### What the Dog Saw: And Other Adventures (2009)
- **Publication Date**: October 2009
- **Background**: Collection of his favorite New Yorker articles
- **Content**: Previously published essays organized by theme

#### David and Goliath: Underdogs, Misfits, and the Art of Battling Giants (2013)
- **Publication Date**: October 2013
- **Background**: Explores how disadvantages can become advantages
- **Themes**: Examines perceived limitations and unexpected strengths

#### Talking to Strangers: What We Should Know about the People We Don't Know (2019)
- **Publication Date**: September 2019
- **Background**: Analyzes miscommunication and misunderstanding
- **Content**: Explores encounters between strangers and their consequences

#### The Bomber Mafia: A Dream, a Temptation, and the Longest Night of the Second World War (2021)
- **Publication Date**: April 2021
- **Background**: Expanded from Revisionist History podcast episodes
- **Content**: Story of WWII bombing strategy and technology
- **Format**: Audio-first approach (audiobook released simultaneously)

#### Revenge of the Tipping Point: Overstories, Superspreaders, and the Rise of Social Engineering (2024)
- **Publication Date**: October 2024
- **Background**: Return to themes from his first book 24 years later
- **Content**: Examines how society has learned to manipulate social epidemics
- **🔴 MILESTONE**: Revisiting the concepts that made him famous

### The American Way of Killing (2026)
- **Announcement**: February 5, 2026
- **Publication Date**: September 29, 2026 (upcoming)
- **Background**: Explores America's gun violence epidemic
- **Status**: Ninth full-length book

## Podcasting and Media (2016-Present)

### Pushkin Industries
- **2018**: Co-founded Pushkin Industries with Jacob Weisberg
  - Audio content company producing podcasts and audiobooks
  - Named after Malcolm's favorite Russian writer, Alexander Pushkin

### Revisionist History
- **June 2016**: Launched Revisionist History podcast
  - Examines overlooked and misunderstood aspects of history
  - Has completed 12 seasons as of early 2026
  - **🔴 MILESTONE**: Pioneering move from print journalism to audio journalism
- **February 2026**: Celebrated 10 years of Revisionist History with "The Big Birthday Party" episode

### Broken Record
- **November 2018**: Launched music podcast with Rick Rubin and Bruce Headlam
  - Interviews with musicians across genres
