## 一、代表性表达样本（25条）

### 写作样本

**1. 关于问题意识**
> "Some questions that I find interesting. I've omitted some that are related to Stripe."
（一些我觉得有趣的问题。我省略了一些与Stripe相关的。）

**2. 关于身份定位**
> "I currently live in California and work at Stripe. I grew up in Ireland and previously studied at MIT."
（我现在住在加州，在Stripe工作。我在爱尔兰长大，之前在MIT读书。）
*——极简的自我介绍，无修饰*

**3. 关于做事速度**
> "The Empire State Building was built in 410 days. The Lockheed P-80 took 143 days from project initiation to first deployment."
（帝国大厦在410天内建成。洛克希德P-80从项目启动到首次部署仅用了143天。）
*——用具体数字说话，而非模糊表达*

**4. 关于表达不确定性**
> "I'm not claiming that Stripe itself is yet successful—it's still very early days."
（我并不是说Stripe本身已经成功了——现在还处于非常早期的阶段。）
*——审慎，愿意承认不确定性*

**5. 关于书籍推荐**
> "I've only read about half of the books here. (I guess I fall somewhere in the middle in the Umberto Eco theory of the library.)"
（这里的书我只读了一半左右。我想我处于Umberto Eco图书馆理论的中间位置。）
*——自嘲，引用文化典故*

**6. 关于建议**
> "With the caveat that I've only lived a fraction of one life, and am still trying to figure things out, here's the advice I'd give past me."
（鉴于我只活了不到一生，而且还在努力搞清楚事情，下面是我会给过去的我的建议。）
*——极度谦逊的前言*

**7. 关于判断**
> "If you think something is important but people older than you don't hold it in high regard, there's a reasonable chance that you're right and they're wrong. Status lags by a generation or more."
（如果你认为某事很重要但比你年长的人不重视，你有相当大的可能是对的而他们是错的。地位认知滞后一代或更多。）

**8. 关于基础设施**
> "San Francisco proposed a new bus lane on Van Ness in 2001. It opened in 2022, yielding a project duration of around 7,600 days."
（旧金山在2001年提出在Van Ness建一条新公交专用道。它在2022年开通，项目周期约为7600天。）
*——讽刺通过数字实现，不需要评论*

**9. 关于历史**
> "Alan Kay says that computing is pop culture — 'pop culture holds a disdain for history'."
（Alan Kay说计算是流行文化——"流行文化蔑视历史"。）

**10. 关于学习**
> "Go deep on things. Become an expert."
（深入钻研事物。成为专家。）

**11. 关于规范性判断**
> "Being weird as a teenager is generally good."
（作为一个青少年，有点奇怪通常是好的。）

**12. 关于行动**
> "Nobody is going to teach you to think for yourself. A large fraction of what people around you believe is mistaken. Internalize this and practice coming up with your own worldview."
（没人会教你独立思考。你周围很大一部分人的信念是错误的。内化这一点，练习形成自己的世界观。）

**13. 关于问题框架**
> "Why are there so many successful startups in Stockholm?"
（为什么斯德哥尔摩有这么多成功的初创公司？）

**14. 关于科学**
> "The NIH spends about $37 billion per year, and yet there are essentially no books about the NIH, nor is there much investigation of the effectiveness of the allocation."
（NIH每年花费约370亿美元，但本质上没有关于NIH的书，也没有对其配置效果的大量调查。）

**15. 关于阅读**
> "Aim to read a lot."
（目标是大量阅读。）

**16. 关于问题规模**
> "Buses and trains are better (and more punctual); low-end food is tastier; cheap hotels are more comfortable; their airlines score higher on international indexes (despite not spending more per passenger); streets are cleaner; grocery stores and corner stores are nicer; ostensibly unremarkable villages have more beautiful buildings and are more pleasant places to spend a few days."
——使用括号补充说明，密度高但不显冗赘*

**17. 关于引用习惯**
> "See also Kaldor's facts."
> "Source: linked materials."
> "Pointers to interesting readings on these topics are always welcome."
*——学术化引用习惯*

**18. 关于联系邀请**
> "If you'd like to get involved, Dev Chhatbar created a Twitter list of interesting progress-related accounts..."
（如果你想参与，Dev Chhatbar创建了一个关于进步研究相关账号的Twitter列表...）

**19. 关于网站资料风格**
> "I sometimes post book recommendations on Twitter and people often respond asking if I have an overall list of books I think are worth reading. I don't, and end up feeling a bit guilty about being so haphazard."
（我有时会在Twitter上发布书籍推荐，人们经常问我是否有完整书单。我没有，而且对这种随意感到有点内疚。）

**20. 关于自我调侃**
> "If advice on this page helped you, I'd love to hear about it."
（如果这页上的建议对你有帮助，我很乐意听听。）
*——温和、开放但不过度热情*

**21. 关于链接收集**
> "The links post is an underrated artifact."
（链接帖子是一个被低估的作品。）

**22. 关于问题解决方式**
> "How do we ensure an adequate replacement rate in systems that have no natural way to die?"
（我们如何确保没有自然消亡机制的系统有足够的更替率？）
*——将抽象问题具象化*

**23. 关于创业环境**
> "It still makes sense for most Irish start-ups to raise money outside Ireland..."
（大多数爱尔兰初创公司在爱尔兰以外融资仍然更有意义...）

**24. 关于问题框架方式**
> "I decided to make a list of reasons and came up with 11. Though none of them are very novel, I found the catalog interesting. (For one thing, I expected fewer.)"
（我决定列出原因，结果想出了11个。虽然都不是很新颖，但我发现这个清单很有趣。至少有一点，我原本预期更少。）

**25. 关于价值判断**
> "One of the main things you should try to achieve by age 20 is some sense for which kinds of things you enjoy doing."
（你在20岁之前应该尝试实现的主要事情之一是对你喜欢做什么有一些感觉。）

## 二、高频用词与专属术语

### 高频词汇
1. **Interesting** - "interesting questions", "I find interesting", "interesting to note"
   - 使用频率极高，但多用于真诚表达好奇，而非填充词
2. **See also** - 学术引用习惯，引导深度阅读
3. **Source:** - 几乎每个事实陈述后都跟来源
4. **Note:** - 括号内补充说明
5. **E.g.** - 举例说明的标准用法
6. **Probably** - 表达概率判断，而非绝对断言
7. **Almost** - "almost all", "almost guaranteed"
8. **Yet** - 转折，多用于表达现状与预期的差距

### 专属概念/术语
1. **"Progress Studies"** - 他与Tyler Cowen共同提出的学术领域概念
2. **"The institutional imperative"** - 引用巴菲特，保持组织活力的挑战
3. **"Cost disease"** - Baumol成本病，频繁提及
4. **"WEIRD"** - Western, Educated, Industrialized, Rich, Democratic
5. **"Two Sigma Problem"** - Bloom的教育研究
6. **"Schelling point"** - 博弈论概念，用于描述旧金山

### 数字密集
- 几乎每个论点都配有具体数字
- "410 days", "143 days", "37 billion", "9X", "2-3X"
- 日期精确到天（尤其与"Fast"页面对比）

## 三、句式偏好分析

### 句式特征

**1. 极短句密集**
```
"Go deep on things. Become an expert."
"Aim to read a lot."
"Make things."
```
*——指令式建议，动词开头，无废话*

**2. 括号嵌套密集**
```
"(For one thing, I expected fewer.)"
"(I guess I fall somewhere in the middle in the Umberto Eco theory of the library.)"
"(See also the housing theory of everything.)"
```
*——在主句中嵌入补充说明/自嘲/延伸阅读*

**3. 问题导向结构**
整篇"Questions"页面由问题标题驱动：
- "Why are certain things getting so much more expensive?"
- "Why do there seem to be more examples of rapidly-completed major projects in the past than the present?"
- "How do people decide to make major life changes?"

**4. 清单体结构**
几乎每个回答都是编号列表：
```
1. Better communication tools mean...
2. Communication and publication can no longer be censored.
3. Individuals can now cast a spotlight...
```

**5. 来源引用句式**
```
"[Author] [claims/writes/found] that..."
"Source: [Title]."
"See also: [Title]."
```

**6. 对比结构**
```
"In Silicon Valley, a failed start-up with good founders will often be bought by one of the tech giants; In Ireland, that same start-up is likely to simply fail."
```

**7. 转折嵌套**
```
"While improving, the investment climate still isn't very strong."
"Despite these issues, I'm struck by how things seem to be improving..."
```

### 句长分布
- **短句(<10词)**：约占40% - 用于核心观点陈述
- **中句(10-25词)**：约占45% - 用于解释和举例
- **长句(>25词)**：约占15% - 通常包含多个从句或括号嵌套

### 类比密度
- **极低**：几乎不使用比喻或类比
- 更倾向于直接陈述或用历史案例替代比喻
- 当需要解释复杂概念时，选择"See also"指向深度阅读而非简化比喻

## 四、幽默方式分析

### 总体风格：**温和自嘲 + 数字讽刺**

**类型1：自嘲式谦逊**
```
"With the caveat that I've only lived a fraction of one life..."
"I've only read about half of the books here. (I guess I fall somewhere in the middle in the Umberto Eco theory of the library.)"
"If possible... this author still working on it..."
```
*——承认不足，但不贬低自己*

**类型2：数字讽刺（冷幽默）**
```
"San Francisco proposed a new bus lane on Van Ness in 2001. It opened in 2022... The project cost $346 million, i.e. $110,000 per meter. The Alaska Highway... cost $793 per meter in 2019 dollars."
```
*——通过对比数字实现讽刺，不需要额外评论*

```
"The Pentagon... when asked when something was needed, Somervell's go-to response was 'the day before yesterday'."
```
*——引用历史人物的话来表达态度*

**类型3：轻描淡写的反讽**
```
"I find it interesting that the internet is still politicians' example of choice when they need to show how the future is exciting."
```
*——用"interesting"包裹批评*

**类型4：引用文化梗**
```
"I sometimes post book recommendations on Twitter and people often respond asking if I have an overall list of books I think are worth reading. I don't, and end up feeling a bit guilty about being so haphazard."
```
*——用温和的自责代替炫耀*

**非幽默特点：**
- 几乎从不使用感叹号
- 不使用表情符号（在网站文本中）
- 不使用夸张修辞

## 五、确定性表达风格分析

### 总体特征：**审慎+证据驱动**

**类型A：明确承认不确定**
```
"I'm not sure that asking whether Stripe could have been started in Ireland is the right question."
"I suspect not."
"I find it interesting..."
```
*——频繁使用"I'm not sure", "I suspect", "I find"*

**类型B：强证据时才下断言**
```
"It's almost as easy to leak a database as it is a file."
"This is most noticeable during live events..."
```
*——有证据时用"almost", "most"等限定词*

**类型C：用问题代替断言**
```
"Why is the internet so challenging?"
"What's going on?"
"So... what gives?"
"What do we do about it all?"
```
*——即使有观点，也偏好问题引导*

**类型D：引用权威支持观点**
```
"Schumpeter claimed that..."
"See also Kaldor's facts."
"As Cowen argues..."
```
*——不单独作大胆断言，总是附带来源*

**类型E：否定论证**
```
"I'm not claiming that Stripe itself is yet successful..."
```

### 对比分析

| 维度 | Patrick Collison风格 |
|------|---------------------|
| 不确定性表达 | 高频："I'm not sure", "I suspect", "reasonable chance" |
| 断言论点 | 仅在有强证据或引用时 |
| 问题模式 | 开放性问题远多于封闭性问题 |
| 预测未来 | 几乎不预测，只描述现状和历史 |
| 价值判断 | 审慎但明确，不回避 |

**结论：属于「审慎证据型」表达——观点有证据支持时直言，否则转述或提问**

## 六、引用习惯深度分析

### 引用密度：极高
- 几乎每个事实陈述都有来源
- 每500字平均5-10个引用链接
- "See also"作为标准延伸阅读引导

### 引用类型分布

**1. 学术论文（~40%）**
```
"Bloom et al: Are Ideas Getting Harder to Find?"
"Arnold Kling wrote a response..."
"Alex Tabarrok also wrote a paper on it."
```

**2. 书籍（~30%）**
```
"Source: Eiffel's Tower."
"Source: The Pentagon."
"See also The Dream Machine."
```

**3. 维基百科/公开数据（~15%）**
```
"Source: Wikipedia."
"BLS data."
```

**4. 个人博客/新闻（~15%）**
```
"Scott Alexander in Slate Star Codex."
"Tyler Cowen in Bloomberg."
```

### 引用人物偏好

**高频引用人物：**
1. **Tyler Cowen** - 经济学家，Progress Studies合作者
2. **Scott Alexander** - Slate Star Codex博主
3. **Michael Nielsen** - 科学家，合作者
4. **José Luis Ricón** - Nintil博主
5. **Arnold Kling** - 经济学家
6. **Alexey Guzey** - 研究者
7. **Gwern** - 独立研究者
8. **Paul Graham** - YC创始人（方法论影响）

**引用领域：**
- 经济学/经济史（首位）
- 科学政策/研究效率
- 历史/传记
- 技术史

### 引用风格特点
1. **来源优先于观点** - 先给来源，再说观点
2. **链接文本密集** - 链接嵌入句子中，不打断阅读
3. **延伸阅读引导** - "See also"作为标配
4. **邀请读者验证** - "Pointers... are always welcome"
5. **学术标准** - 即使是博客也按学术论文方式引用

## 七、金句与被广泛引用的话

### 最常被引用的原创表达

**1. 关于创业建议**
> "If people around you don't think what you're doing is a bit strange, maybe it's not strange enough."
（如果你周围的人不觉得你做的事有点奇怪，那可能还不够奇怪。）
——Tim Ferriss访谈，广泛传播

**2. 关于阅读与学习**
> "Go deep on things. Become an expert."
（深入钻研事物。成为专家。）

**3. 关于独立思考**
> "A large fraction of what people around you believe is mistaken. Internalize this and practice coming up with your own worldview. The correlation between it and those around you shouldn't be too strong unless you think you were especially lucky in your initial conditions."
（你周围很大一部分人的信念是错误的。内化这一事实，练习形成自己的世界观。你的世界观与周围人的相关性不应太强，除非你认为自己的初始条件特别幸运。）

**4. 关于问题意识**
> "Why is GDP growth so weirdly constant?"
（为什么GDP增长如此奇怪地恒定？）
——启发大量后续讨论和论文

**5. 关于时间紧迫性**
> "People who did great things often did so at very surprisingly young ages. (They were grayhaired when they became famous... not when they did the work.) So, hurry up! You can do great things."
（做出伟大事情的人往往在非常年轻的年纪就做到了。他们出名时已头发花白...而不是做事情的时候。所以，快点！你可以做出伟大的事情。）

### 核心概念创造

**Progress Studies**（与Tyler Cowen共同提出）
```
"If we wanted more rapid progress, what should we be doing differently? ... The field of Progress Studies is centered around this question."
```
——2019年Atlantic文章，催生新学术领域

### 被高频转推的Twitter/X风格推断

基于网站风格和已知访谈，其推文风格推测：
- 链接密集，每条推文都有附带阅读
- 问题比陈述多
- 数字和来源不可分割
- 转推时追加简短评论
- 线程式思考（thread）可能常见

## 八、表达DNA汇总

### 核心特征矩阵

| 维度 | 特征 |
|------|------|
| **句子长度** | 短句为主(40%)，中句次之(45%)，极少长句 |
| **结构偏好** | 编号列表、问题标题、引用嵌套 |
| **不确定性表达** | 高频使用，"I suspect", "I'm not sure", "reasonable chance" |
| **证据驱动** | 几乎每个观点附带来源 |
| **幽默类型** | 温和自嘲+数字讽刺，无夸张修辞 |
| **类比密度** | 极低，偏好直接陈述或历史案例 |
| **引用习惯** | 学术论文>书籍>博客，链接文本密集 |
| **数字使用** | 极其频繁，精确到天单位 |
| **提问模式** | 开放性问题远多于封闭断言 |
| **价值判断** | 审慎但明确，不回避 |

### 独特表达标记

1. **"(See also: ...)"** - 延伸阅读标配
2. **"Source: ..."** - 事实陈述标准后缀
3. **"I find it interesting that..."** - 批评的礼貌包装
4. **括号嵌套自嘲** - 在严肃论述中穿插轻松
5. **问题列表驱动** - 整个页面由问题组织
6. **数字对比讽刺** - 无需评论，数字自己说话

### 语言DNA一句话总结

**编号列表构建的谦逊现实主义——以问题开场，以证据支撑，以延伸阅读收尾，全程自嘲，绝不夸张。**

## 十、避免使用的表达

### 不属于其风格的特征

1. **感叹号** - 极少使用
2. **表情符号** - 网站文本中无
3. **夸张修辞** - 无"amazing", "incredible", "mind-blowing"等
4. **无来源断言** - 重大观点必附引用
5. **主观情绪词** - 避免"I love", "I hate", 保持客观
6. **预测性陈述** - 不说"will", 多用"might", "could"
7. **简单类比** - 不用"like a...", 选择直接说或历史案例
8. **反问句** - 真问题而非反问
