### 2. DeepLearning.AI 直播对话: Andrew Ng 与 Yann LeCun 讨论 AI 暂停提案 (2023)

**来源**: https://deepleaps.com/misc/andrew-ng-and-yann-lecun-address-ai-dangers-and-debate-proposed-six-month-moratorium-on-ai-development-during-live-stream/
**可信度**: 高（DeepLearning.AI 官方直播）

**背景**: 2023年，Future Life Institute 发布公开信呼吁暂停AI开发6个月，Andrew Ng 和 Yann LeCun 在直播中讨论此事

**被追问时的回答方式**（如何应对挑战）:

**片段1 - 对AI暂停提案的回应**:
> *"While there are risks of harm associated with AI, such as bias, fairness, and concentration of power, AI is also creating tremendous value in fields like education, healthcare, and responsive coaching. The development of even more advanced AI models would help a vast number of people and pausing progress would slow down the creation of valuable applications."*

**立场一致性分析**:
- Andrew Ng 明确反对暂停提案，但不是简单否定
- 他承认风险存在（偏见、公平性、权力集中），但强调价值创造
- 这种"承认问题 + 但主张前进"的模式是他标志性的回答方式

**片段2 - 与Yann LeCun的共识与分歧**:
> *"Both LeCun and Ng agree on the importance of making AI systems controllable and ensuring their accuracy in providing information."*
>
> *"Both speakers believe that the hype around AI 'doomsaying' is another form of harmful exaggeration, as it creates unrealistic expectations about AI's capabilities."*

**争议话题的态度**:

**关于AI取代工作**:
> *"AI software will be in direct competition with a lot of people for jobs. That's something Silicon Valley needs to own up to."*
> ——Stanford 演讲（2017）

他承认AI会造成工作替代，但同时强调：
> *"With every major wave of technology innovation, from the steam engine to electricity to the computer, the technology has created far more jobs than it has destroyed."*

**关于AGI风险**:
> *"If AI were truly close to human-level intelligence, we would have level five autonomous driving, which we do not."*
>
> *"He uses the example of a teenager learning to drive in 20 hours of training, highlighting that we still do not have self-driving cars despite the advancements in AI."*

**拒绝回答的问题边界**:
- 在讨论LeCun的"Don't Fear the Terminator"文章时，Andrew Ng表示认同但补充了自己的观点
- 他没有明确拒绝回答问题，但对某些假设性问题会让步说"这很模糊"或"没有人确切知道"

### 3. Davos 2025: AGI Panel 讨论

**来源**: https://www.rcrwireless.com/20250204/uncategorized/agi-at-davos-risk-control-ai-infrastructure-arms-race/
**可信度**: 高（世界经济论坛官方记录）

**Andrew Ng vs Yoshua Bengio 激烈交锋**:

这是Andrew Ng最激烈的公开辩论之一。Yoshua Bengio 直接批评他的观点：

> *"I saw several things Ng said are 'deadly wrong.'"*
> ——Yoshua Bengio

**被追问时如何应对挑战（实时辩论案例）**:

**Bengio的质疑**:
> *"AI is already displaying emergent behaviors that suggest it could develop its own agency, making its control far from guaranteed... These [behaviors] were not programmed. These are emerging."*
>
> *"Today, we don't know how to control machines that are as smart as us. If we don't figure it out, do you understand the consequences?"*

**Andrew Ng的反驳方式**（值得注意的策略）:
> *"AI systems learn from human data, and humans can engage in deceptive behavior. If you can get an AI to demonstrate that, it'll be controlled and stopped."*

**反驳策略分析**:
1. **不否认问题的存在** - 承认AI可能学到人类的不良行为
2. **转框架** - 将"AI失控"转化为"可检测、可控制"的技术问题
3. **类比** - 使用飞机行业的比喻（见下文）

**他的经典类比**:
> *"Every year, our ability to control AI is improving. I think the safest way to make sure AI doesn't do bad things is the same way we build airplanes. Sometimes something bad happens, and we fix it."*

**情感表达**:
- 在这场辩论中，Andrew Ng 表现得冷静坚定
- 当Bengio使用"deadly wrong"这样强烈的批评时，他没有情绪化，而是用技术论据回应
- 他的语气始终是"工程师解决问题"的风格，而非"辩论赛"风格

**最终立场**:
> *"The net benefits massively outweigh the risks."*

### 4. Stanford Graduate School of Business: Why AI Is the New Electricity (2017)

**来源**: https://www.gsb.stanford.edu/insights/andrew-ng-why-ai-new-electricity
**可信度**: 高（Stanford GSB 官方）

**核心类比（反复使用的比喻）**:

> *"Just as electricity transformed almost everything 100 years ago, today I actually have a hard time thinking of an industry that I don't think AI will transform in the next several years."*

**这个"AI是新时代的电力"类比是他最著名的说法，几乎是他的个人标签**

**争议话题的态度**:

**关于"邪恶AI"**:
> *"I think that there is no clear path to how AI can become sentient. If it does, it might take hundreds or thousands of years. Worrying about evil AI killer robots today is a little bit like worrying about overpopulation on the planet Mars."*

**比喻特点**:
- 使用"火星人口过剩"这种荒诞但有效的比喻
- 不直接否定恐惧，而是用幽默的方式淡化
- 将遥远的问题转化为"现在不必担心"的结论

**关于AI取代工作**（他承认这是真正的问题）:
> *"The real concern regarding AI is societal impact. Evil AI hype is being used to whitewash a much more serious issue, which is job displacement."*

**他说硅谷需要"own up to"这个问题**:
> *"I think government should give people a safety net, but pay the unemployed to study, to provide the structure to help the unemployed study, so as to increase the odds of gaining skills needed to re-enter the workforce."*

**政策主张**:
- 政府应该提供安全网
- 但要求失业者学习新技能作为交换
- 这体现了他"技术乐观 + 社会责任"的双重立场

### 5. TED Talk: AI Isn't the Problem — It's the Solution (2023)

**来源**: https://www.ted.com/talks/andrew_ng_ai_isn_t_the_problem_it_s_the_solution
**转录来源**: https://blog.csdn.net/shizheng_Li/article/details/137429075
**可信度**: 高（TED官方）

**开场（情感表达 - 自嘲与共情）**:

> *"I like to be a cheerleader for AI, not a critic. I think that, like electricity, AI will advance nearly every human endeavor. But I'm sympathetic to the people that find this technology unsettling. Humor me for a minute. I'd like to step into the shoes of the critics, because I think they make some thoughtful points, and I want everyone to understand these points."*

**表达特点**:
- 先表明自己的立场（AI支持者）
- 然后用"Humor me for a minute"邀请观众跟随他的思路
- "step into the shoes of the critics" - 展现愿意理解对立观点的态度
- 这是他标志性的"先承认、再反驳"技巧

**即兴类比和比喻（TED演讲中密集使用）**:

**比喻1: AI与电力**
> *"AI is the new electricity. And it is poised to revolutionize every industry and every corner of human life."*
>
> *"Many of the fears about AI today are similar to the fears about electricity when that was new. People were terrified about electrocution or about electricity sparking devastating fires. Today, electricity still has its dangers, but I think few of us would give up light, heat and refrigeration for fear of electrocution."*

**比喻2: 飞机安全**
> *"No one can fully control an airplane today. It's buffeted around by winds and turbulence, and the pilot may make a mistake. In the early days of aviation, airplanes killed a lot of people. But we learned from those experiences and built safer aircraft and devised better rules by which to operate them, so that today most of us can step into an airplane without fearing for our lives."*

**比喻3: 放射科医生**
> *"AI won't replace radiologists, but radiologists that use AI will replace radiologists that don't."*
> ——引用自 Stanford 放射科教授 Curt Langlotz

**比喻4: 智能作为服务**
> *"AI opens up the potential for every individual to hire intelligence inexpensively, so that you no longer have to worry about that huge bill from going to see a doctor for falling sick, or for getting an education. And you be able to hire an army of smart, well-intentioned, well-informed staff to help you think things through."*

**技术讨论（展示专业知识）**:

在讨论RLHF（Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback）时：
> *"I'm going to get a little technical here, because I want everyone to understand exactly how this works, because I think with that understanding AI can seem less scary."*

**这句话体现了他对技术解释的坚持**:
- 即使是面向大众的TED演讲，他也愿意深入技术细节
- 理由是"理解可以减少恐惧"
- 这与他一贯的教育者身份一致

**对批评者的态度**:
> *"Earlier this year, about 34,000 people, including some prominent scientists, signed a declaration calling for a pause in advanced AI research. But I'm here to tell you that this anxiety is misplaced. I don't think we need less AI. We need much more."*

**直接反驳但不攻击个人**:
- 提到签名人数和"著名科学家"
- 但不点名，避免人身攻击
- 只针对观点，不针对人

### 6. Wharton Knowledge Interview: How MOOCs Are Taking Local Knowledge Global (2015)

**来源**: https://knowledge.wharton.upenn.edu/article/how-moocs-are-taking-local-knowledge-global/
**可信度**: 高（Wharton 官方）

**情感表达 - 讲故事的温情时刻**:

> *"I kicked off a Coursera founders' conference by telling the story of one of the students, a baker in Bangladesh. She took Coursera courses — including a microeconomics class from the University of Pennsylvania and model thinking from the University of Michigan — and learned how to run a business. I showed her statements of accomplishments using her verified certificates on the big screen at the Coursera founders' conference. Here was a woman who could never attend classes in her city, but today she credits part of her success to Coursera courses."*

**表达特点**:
- 用具体人物故事打动听众
- 展现他对教育改变命运的真诚信念
- 这是他在讨论教育话题时最常流露情感的时刻

**被追问时的回答方式（关于完成率）**:

**问题**: MOOCs的被质疑的一点是完成率低

**他的回应**:
> *"You have 5,000 students that complete a course. There's about an equal number that watches every single video, but doesn't do the homework. So should we then say that the completion rates are actually almost double what they are? If they're watching every single video, presumably they're getting something out of it, but they just chose not to do the homework. That seems like a fine thing to me."*

**回应策略**:
1. **重定义问题** - 让观众重新思考"完成率"的定义
2. **不可辩驳的逻辑** - 看完视频却不做作业，依然有价值
3. **不强求完美** - "That seems like a fine thing to me"

**继续追问时的开放态度**:
> *"At Coursera, we are starting to experiment with more flexible models of education. Today we're asking ourselves questions such as, 'For this region or instruction, why do we need deadlines?'... And that's a really good question; I really don't know the answer to that. It's possible that without deadlines, maybe no one will complete courses. My instinct is that won't be the case, but these are the sorts of questions we're asking ourselves."*

**表达特点**:
- 坦诚承认"我不知道答案"
- 分享自己的"直觉"但强调是推测
- 展现持续思考的态度

**关于应对批评（发展中国家vs发达国家）**:

**批评**: Coursera主要服务发达国家用户

**他的回应**:
> *"I think everyone will agree that's a fantastic thing. And that is actually what Coursera does today. It's just that on top of that, we serve another five-and-a-half million learners that come from developed economies."*

**回应策略**:
- "先承认，再反转" - 是的，我们也服务发达国家，但这是我们做的"额外"的事
- 不辩解，而是重新框架：做得多不是错

### 2. 类比使用模式

**重复使用模式**:
- "AI是电力"被他反复使用超过5年在不同的场合

- 这是他建立个人品牌的核心比喻

**类比举证策略**:
- 从已知到未知：用观众熟悉的事物（电力、飞机、医生）解释不熟悉的AI
- 从技术到社会：先解释技术原理，再扩展到社会影响
- 从承认到反转："是的，电力有危险...但没有人愿意放弃"

## 四、争议话题的态度

### 1. AI安全性

**立场**: 不认为AI存在根本性的"邪恶"风险，但承认工程问题

> *"The flaws of AI today, I think, are engineering problems to be solved, rather than a fundamental evil force that must be stopped."*
> ——TED, 2023

**与Yann LeCun的共识**:
> *"The scenarios where AI systems dominate humanity are unrealistic. This is because the motivation to dominate exists in humans and social species, but there is no reason for it to exist in AI systems that we design."*

### 2. AI取代工作

**立场**: 承认会有工作流失，但整体利好

> *"AI won't replace radiologists, but radiologists that use AI will replace radiologists that don't."*

**不淡化问题，但提供解决方案**:
> *"I don't mean to minimize the challenge of helping many people adopt AI, or the suffering of the much smaller number of people whose jobs will disappear, or our responsibility to make sure they have a safety net and that they have an opportunity to gain new skills."*
> ——TED, 2023

### 3. AI监管

**立场**: 支持监管，但反对过度限制研发

> *"More constructive proposals could include increased research on AI safety, transparency, auditing, and more public funding for basic AI research."*
>
> *"It would be a terrible innovation policy for governments to pass legislation slowing down the progress of technology, especially when they may not fully understand it."*
> ——DeepLearning.AI直播, 2023

**核心主张**: 监管应用，而非技术本身

### 4. AI开源

**立场**: 支持开源

> *"Andrew Ng has been a steadfast advocate for open source AI and believes that regulations should target specific applications rather than the technology itself."*
> ——采访总结, 2024

## 五、情感表达分析

### 1. 什么时候会激动

**讲述教育改变命运的故事时**:

> *"When I do this in developing economies, one thing that struck me was the real hunger for knowledge. In the U.S. when I go to some meet ups, there is a certain hunger for knowledge. When we walk around our university campuses, we are used to information overload, there is just a buffet of great causes, and we just don't have time to learn everything we could learn. In developing economies, these people really don't have access. I'm very surprised when people would take a train for two hours to listen to people talk in some city because in the rural towns in which they live they just did not have access to knowledge. When I speak with all these learners about MOOCs, there is a certain gratitude and hunger that's different than what there is in the developed world."*
> ——Wharton采访, 2015

**情感特征**:
- 多次使用"gratitude"、"hunger"等情感词汇
- 坦然表达"surprised"
- 语速变慢，细节增多（"两小时火车"）

### 2. 什么时候幽默

**使用荒诞比喻淡化恐惧时**:

> *"Worrying about evil AI killer robots today is a little bit like worrying about overpopulation on the planet Mars."*
> ——Stanford演讲, 2017

**幽默特征**:
- 不是大笑式幽默，而是基于逻辑荒诞的轻幽默
- 用"大家都知道这是荒谬的"来证明观点
- 避免直接嘲笑反对者

**TED演讲中的开场**:
> *"Humor me for a minute."*
>
> 这是一个典型的"英式"表达，既不是玩笑也不是命令，而是一种邀请

### 3. 什么时候犹豫

**在承认不确定性时**:

> *"It's possible that without deadlines, maybe no one will complete courses. My instinct is that won't be the case, but these are the sorts of questions we're asking ourselves."*
> ——Wharton采访, 2015

**犹豫特征**:
- 明确说"I don't know the answer"
- 承认"My instinct"（直觉）而非知识
- 使用"it's possible"、"maybe"等限定词

**面对无法反驳的批评时**:

在Davos辩论中，当Bengio说"deadly wrong"时，他的回应：
> *"I saw several things Ng said are 'deadly wrong.'... He argued that AI is on a trajectory toward developing its own goals and behaviors."*

Andrew Ng没有直接回应"deadly wrong"的措辞，而是说：
> *"AI systems learn from human data, and humans can engage in deceptive behavior."*

**应对策略**: 不纠结于批评的措辞，而是回应批评的实质

### 4. 什么时候坚定

**捍卫自己的核心信念时**:

在Davos 2025面对多位反对者：
> *"The net benefits massively outweigh the risks."*

**在DeepLearning.AI直播中讨论暂停AI**:
> *"I think that the proposal for a six-month pause in AI development is not the right approach."*

**在TED演讲的结尾**:
> *"I think for all of us to address and solve these global problems, we will need all the intelligence – including all the artificial intelligence – we can muster."*

## 六、回答方式模式总结

### 1. 标准回答结构

**模式一：承认 + 重框架 + 反驳**
> "Yes, AI has risks [承认]... But these are engineering problems [重框架]... Not fundamental evil to be stopped [反驳]."

**模式二：类比 + 现实案例 + 结论**
> "Like electricity [类比]... People feared electrocution [案例]... But few would give up light [结论]."

**模式三：承认不确定 + 给出直觉 + 邀请继续讨论**
> "I really don't know the answer to that [承认不确定]... My instinct is X [给出直觉]... These are the questions we're asking ourselves [邀请讨论]."

### 2. 不做的事

- **不贬低批评者**: 即使不同意，也会先"step into the shoes of the critics"
- **不使用绝对化语言**: 很少说"绝不会"或"肯定会"，更常用"我认为不太可能"
- **不回应人身攻击**: 即使被说"deadly wrong"，也只回应观点
- **不假装知道**: 对于真正不确定的问题，会直接说"I don't know"

### 3. 情感控制

**始终保持的基调**:
- 教育者的耐心
- 工程师的务实
- 乐天派的信念
